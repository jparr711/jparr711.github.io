Small Area Estimation for Site Selection in Zambia

For this project, I utilized small area estimation models to create subnational estimates for key indicators to inform district selection for the Zambia Local Impact program funded by USAID. Program leaders wanted to target areas with low socioeconomic development in the provinces of Central and Muchinga, however several relevant indicators were not available at the district level. To overcome this limitation, we linked the Demographic Health Survey with the Census using multilevel regression with post-stratification (MRP) to produce estimates of female literacy rates and child stunting rates at the district level. These indicators were then combined with others to form a composite selection score to select districts to receive programmatic support.

Mexico Clean Energy Jobs

This dashboard was built to analyze trends in employment in the energy sector in Mexico for the Mexico Clean Energy (EpiEnergia) project funded by FCDO/UK. The data process involved three steps: (1) building webscrapers in Python to pull data from job postings from different sites, (2) data processing to combine data from difference sources and save into PostgreSQL database, and; (3) developing an R/Shiny dashboard (no longer active) to highlight trends in employment in the clean energy and traditional oil & gas sectors.

Current Projects

Measuring Food Insecurity

The purpose of this project is to refine use of psychometric diagnostics and calibration methods to calculate the prevalence of food insecurity across multiple rounds of Feed the Future household surveys. The Food Insecurity Experience Scale (FIES) is an 8-item series of binary questions regarding people's access to adequate food. The Surveys for Monitoring Resilience & Food Security project funded by USAID uses this scale in population-based surveys to measure moderate and severe food security prevalence in Feed the Future target zones.

Predicting Rental Values using Machine Learning in Low and Middle Income Countries

Housing expenditure is a key component of aggregate consumption expenditure used to estimate the welfare status and poverty status of households, particularly in international development. However, how does one estimate rental values of households for consumption aggregate surveys when information on rents is not collected or is missing? Standard guidelines as outlined suggest four main methods to impute rental-value of owner-occupied dwelling: self-reporting (or self-assessment), hedonic regression, rent-to-value, and user-cost methods. We expand the utility of these methods by incorporating two novel approaches to estimating rental values: geospatial information and machine learning models.
