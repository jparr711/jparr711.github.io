Mexico Clean Energy Jobs

This analytical dashboard was built to assess jobs in the energy sector in Mexico for the Mexico Clean Energy (EpiEnergia) project funded by the Foreign Commonwealth Development Office (FCDO) of the United Kingdom. The data process involved three steps: (1) building webscrapers in Python to pull data from job postings from different sites, (2) data processing to combine data from difference sources and save into PostgreSQL database, and; (3) developing an R/Shiny dashboard (no longer active) to highlight trends in employment in the clean energy and traditional oil & gas sectors.

Current Projects

Measuring Food Insecurity

The Food Insecurity Experience Scale (FIES) is an 8-item series of binary questions regarding people's access to adequate food. The Surveys for Monitoring Resilience & Food Security project funded by USAID used this scale in population-based surveys to measure moderate and severe food security prevalence in Feed the Future target zones. This work involved defining appropriate diagnostics to validate the quality of the scale for a given survey, procedures to properly measure prevalence rates by calibrating them against the global reference scale, and how to equate surveys taken at different rounds.

Predicting Rental Values using Machine Learning in Low and Middle Income Countries

Housing expenditure is a key component of aggregate consumption expenditure used to estimate the welfare status and poverty status of households, particularly in international development. However, how does one estimate rental values of households for consumption aggregate surveys when information on rents is not collected or is missing? Standard guidelines as outlined suggest four main methods to impute rental-value of owner-occupied dwelling: self-reporting (or self-assessment), hedonic regression, rent-to-value, and user-cost methods. We expand the utility of these methods by incorporating two novel approaches to estimating rental values: geospatial information and machine learning models.
